Backlog

The Backlog holds actions that are real but not yet the right time. They're not forgotten — they're deliberately deferred.

Moving an action to the Backlog is a decision: this is real work, but I'm not committing to it right now. This keeps your Next Actions list short and trustworthy — everything on it is something you've genuinely committed to.

Use the Backlog for:

  • Actions that depend on something else finishing first
  • Seasonal tasks (winterise the garden — not relevant in spring)
  • Things you want to do eventually but not urgently

During your weekly review, scan the Backlog and promote anything that's now ready. Move it to Next status so it appears in your active list.

Age column

Each row shows an Age value — how long since the action was last touched. A backlog item that has been sitting for months is a candidate for deletion or promotion during your next review.

Due column

If an action has a due date set, the Due column shows approaching pressure — overdue in red, today in amber, this week as a day name, later as a short date. Open any action (double-click or pencil icon) to set or clear its due date.

Sort order

The Sort button cycles through three states:

  • Sort (inactive) — your manual order is preserved
  • Oldest — oldest items first; useful for clearing forgotten work
  • Newest — most recent first

When a sort is active, the up/down ordering buttons are hidden. The sort preference is saved across restarts.
